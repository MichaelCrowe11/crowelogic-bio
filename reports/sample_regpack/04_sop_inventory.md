# SOP: Schedule I Inventory and Record-Keeping

**Document number:** SOP-REG-001
**Effective date:** 2026-04-21
**Facility:** Crowe Logic Psychedelic Research Laboratory
**Applicable registration:** Crowe Logic, Inc. — Michael B. Crowe
**Regulatory basis:** 21 CFR 1304.11 (initial & biennial inventory), 21 CFR 1304.21 (records), 21 CFR 1304.22 (receipts/dispositions)

---

## 1. Purpose

This SOP establishes the procedure for recording, tracking, and
reconciling all Schedule I controlled substances received, stored,
dispensed, and destroyed at this facility.

## 2. Scope

Applies to all personnel authorized to handle Schedule I substances at
Crowe Logic Psychedelic Research Laboratory under DEA registration issued to
Michael B. Crowe, .

## 3. Responsibilities

| Role | Responsibility |
|---|---|
| Principal Investigator | Overall compliance; signs biennial inventory |
| Alternate Responsible Individual | Backup for PI; maintains continuity |
| Authorized laboratory personnel | Daily dispensing records; substance handling |
| Designated records custodian | Maintains inventory logs on-site for DEA inspection |

## 4. Procedure

### 4.1 Receipt of Schedule I substances

1. Upon delivery, verify the shipment matches the purchase order and
   the invoice from the DEA-registered source.
2. Record in the Receipt Log:
   - Date of receipt
   - Substance name and DEA schedule
   - Source (supplier name and DEA number)
   - Quantity received (mg or mL, with concentration)
   - Lot number
   - Invoice/PO reference number
   - Initials of receiving personnel and witness
3. Immediately transfer the substance to the Schedule I storage safe.
4. Retain the invoice for a minimum of two (2) years (21 CFR 1304.04).

### 4.2 Initial inventory (21 CFR 1304.11)

Within two business days of DEA registration issuance, the Principal
Investigator shall take a physical inventory of all Schedule I substances
on hand. The inventory shall include:

- Substance name
- Finished form (e.g., 10 mg capsules, 5 mg/mL solution)
- Total quantity on hand
- Date of inventory

The initial inventory shall be signed and dated by the PI and maintained
at the facility.

### 4.3 Biennial inventory (21 CFR 1304.11(c))

A physical inventory shall be taken on or as of **the same date as the
initial inventory** every two years. The biennial inventory may be taken
on any date within two years of the previous inventory, provided it
does not exceed two years from the prior.

### 4.4 Daily dispensing record

Each time a Schedule I substance is removed from storage for use in
research activities, the dispensing personnel shall record:

- Date and time of removal
- Substance name
- Quantity removed (with units)
- Protocol/experiment identifier
- Intended use (1-line description)
- Initials of dispensing personnel and witness (if applicable)
- Quantity returned to storage (if any) and reconciliation notes

The daily dispensing record shall be maintained in a bound logbook OR
in an electronic system with audit trail and printable export capability.

### 4.5 Substance-specific tracking

For each compound covered by this registration, maintain a per-substance
running balance sheet:

| Date | Action | Qty change (mg) | Running balance (mg) | Initials |
|---|---|---|---|---|

Opening balance entries for this registration:

- **Psilocybin**: estimated opening balance 0 mg (to be received from Cayman Chemical (DEA-registered supplier) — analytical standard)
- **Psilocin**: estimated opening balance 0 mg (to be received from Cayman Chemical — analytical standard)
- **DMT**: estimated opening balance 0 mg (to be received from Cayman Chemical — analytical standard)


### 4.6 Reconciliation

At minimum weekly, the designated records custodian shall reconcile:

- Running balance per substance (from 4.5)
- Physical quantity in storage (by weight or volume)
- Dispensing record entries (from 4.4)

Discrepancies greater than 2% of the expected balance shall be
immediately reported to the Principal Investigator and investigated
within 24 hours. Unresolved discrepancies that may indicate loss or
theft trigger the Emergency Response procedure (`06_sop_emergency.md`).

## 5. Record retention (21 CFR 1304.04)

All records required by 21 CFR 1304 shall be maintained **on-site** for
a minimum of **two (2) years** from the date of the record. Records
shall be made immediately available upon DEA inspection.

Records shall be stored in a manner that protects them from loss,
alteration, or unauthorized access (locked file cabinet OR electronic
system with access controls).

## 6. Quality control

- Inventory logs shall be reviewed by the PI or Alternate monthly
- Any corrections to a logbook entry shall be made by striking through
  the erroneous entry (so it remains legible), writing the correct
  entry, and initialing and dating the correction
- Electronic records must have an unalterable audit trail
