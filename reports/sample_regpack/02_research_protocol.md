# Research Protocol Summary

**Protocol title:** Structure-activity relationships of 5-HT2A agonist tryptamines: receptor binding, functional selectivity, and analytical characterization

**Principal Investigator:** Michael B. Crowe, 
**Institution:** Crowe Logic, Inc.

---

## 1. Research objective

Characterize receptor binding affinity, functional selectivity (Gαq vs β-arrestin2 bias), and ADMET properties of a set of psilocin-related tryptamines to inform rational design of non-hallucinogenic 5-HT2A agonists with antidepressant potential. All work is conducted in vitro using heterologously expressed 5-HT2A receptor preparations and analytical instrumentation.

## 3. Study design





### In vitro / analytical

This protocol is limited to in vitro and analytical work. Activities
include:

- Receptor binding assays (radioligand or fluorescent)
- Functional assays (cAMP, β-arrestin recruitment, calcium flux)
- Analytical chemistry (HPLC, LC-MS, NMR) for compound identity and purity
- Stability and formulation studies

No human or animal administration is authorized under this registration.
Any extension to human or animal work will require protocol amendment
and — if Schedule I — notification to the DEA.
