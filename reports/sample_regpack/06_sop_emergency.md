# SOP: Emergency Response — Theft, Loss, or Breach

**Document number:** SOP-REG-003
**Effective date:** 2026-04-21
**Facility:** Crowe Logic Psychedelic Research Laboratory
**Regulatory basis:** 21 CFR 1301.74(c), 21 CFR 1301.76(b), 21 CFR 1301.91, DEA Form 106

---

## 1. Purpose

Define response to theft, loss, diversion, or breach of the Schedule I
storage area. Speed matters: DEA requires notification **within one (1)
business day** of discovery of any significant loss or theft.

## 2. Triggers for this SOP

Invoke this SOP if any of the following occur:

- Physical break-in or evidence of tampering at the Schedule I storage
- Discrepancy in substance quantity > 2% of expected balance that
  cannot be resolved within 24 hours
- Alarm activation with no identified authorized cause
- Loss of keys, combination, or access credential without rapid recovery
- Evidence or suspicion of diversion by personnel
- Any substance-related medical incident involving an unauthorized person

## 3. Immediate actions (within 1 hour of discovery)

### 3.1 Secure the scene

- **Stop all activity** in the Schedule I storage area
- **Do not move or alter** any evidence of tampering or breach
- **Lock the facility** and preserve all physical evidence

### 3.2 Notify the Principal Investigator

The Principal Investigator **Michael B. Crowe**
(phone: +1-480-322-5761, email: michael@crowelogic.com)
must be notified immediately, regardless of hour or day.

### 3.3 Contact law enforcement (theft suspected)

If theft is suspected or confirmed:

1. Call local police/sheriff and request a formal report
2. Obtain the police report number
3. Document time of call, officer name, and report number

## 4. Actions within 1 business day

### 4.1 Submit DEA Form 106

DEA Form 106 (Report of Theft or Loss of Controlled Substances) must be
filed online at **deadiversion.usdoj.gov/webforms2/Forms/Form106.pdf**
or the current electronic submission system within **one business day**
of discovery of a significant loss or theft.

Include:

- Applicant DEA registration number
- Date, time, place of discovery
- Description of the substance(s) involved
- Quantity lost or stolen
- Circumstances of the loss or theft
- Local law enforcement report number (if applicable)
- Actions taken to prevent recurrence

Retain a copy of the submitted Form 106 at the facility.

### 4.2 Institutional notification

Notify:

- Institutional biosafety officer or compliance office
- Institutional legal counsel
- Institutional Animal Care / IRB office (if the loss affects ongoing
  protocols)
- Insurance carrier (if applicable)

## 5. Actions within 7 days

### 5.1 Root cause analysis

Complete a written root cause analysis identifying:

- How the incident occurred
- Why existing controls did not prevent it
- What control changes will prevent recurrence
- Training gaps, if any
- Whether the security plan (`03_security_plan.md`) requires revision

### 5.2 Corrective actions

Implement and document corrective actions. Typical actions include:

- Rekey or reset combination of storage container
- Revise personnel access list
- Update alarm system zones or sensitivity
- Add or relocate surveillance cameras
- Retrain all authorized personnel
- Revise applicable SOPs

## 6. Definition: significant loss or theft

DEA guidance (and 21 CFR 1301.76(b)) does not provide a bright-line
percentage. Factors include:

- Schedule of the substance (Schedule I/II given more scrutiny)
- Actual quantity lost vs expected handling patterns
- Evidence of criminal activity
- Pattern (isolated incident vs recurring discrepancies)
- History of the registrant

**When in doubt, report.** Under-reporting carries substantially more
risk than over-reporting.

## 7. Cooperation with DEA investigation

If DEA initiates an inspection or investigation following an incident:

- Preserve all records related to the incident
- Preserve physical evidence (container, lock, damaged areas)
- Provide DEA investigators with requested records promptly
- Do not modify SOPs, logs, or security systems without DEA concurrence
  during an active investigation (except as required for ongoing safety)

[LEGAL REVIEW] Institutional counsel should be present or consulted for
all DEA communications following a reportable incident.

## 8. Prevention

### 8.1 Daily checks

- Verify storage area is secure at start and end of workday
- Verify alarm system status daily
- Spot-check inventory weekly (see `04_sop_inventory.md` §4.6)

### 8.2 Training

All authorized personnel shall receive training on this emergency SOP
upon designation and annually thereafter. Training records shall be
maintained per 21 CFR 1301.75.
