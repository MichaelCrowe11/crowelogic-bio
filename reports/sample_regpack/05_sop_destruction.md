# SOP: Schedule I Substance Destruction

**Document number:** SOP-REG-002
**Effective date:** 2026-04-21
**Facility:** Crowe Logic Psychedelic Research Laboratory
**Regulatory basis:** 21 CFR 1317.05 (destruction), 21 CFR 1317.15 (non-registrants), 21 CFR 1304.21(e), DEA Form 41

---

## 1. Purpose

Define the procedure for destruction of Schedule I substances that are
expired, no longer needed, contaminated, or otherwise unfit for use.
Destruction must render the substance **non-retrievable** (21 CFR
1300.05) before disposal.

## 2. Non-retrievable standard (21 CFR 1300.05)

"Non-retrievable" means permanently altered so the substance cannot be
transformed back to its original or any other controlled substance. The
DEA does not prescribe a specific method; acceptable methods include:

- Incineration at a permitted facility
- Chemical denaturation followed by disposal as hazardous waste
- Dissolution in a denaturing solvent that cannot be reversed

Flushing down the drain is **not** acceptable under EPA pharmaceutical
waste rules (40 CFR 261) and is separately prohibited by most state
environmental agencies.

## 3. Authority to destroy

Destruction of Schedule I substances requires:

1. Advance approval from the Principal Investigator and documentation
   of the rationale (expiration, contamination, protocol completion, etc.)
2. A DEA-registered witness at the time of destruction (typically the
   Alternate Responsible Individual or another authorized DEA registrant)
3. Completion of **DEA Form 41** (Registrants Inventory of Drugs
   Surrendered) — signed by both the destroyer and witness
4. Destruction at a DEA-approved disposal method or transfer to a
   DEA-registered reverse distributor

[LEGAL REVIEW] On-site destruction by a researcher requires the
`non-retrievable` method to be pre-approved by the DEA Diversion field
office. Counsel should confirm whether the institution plans to perform
on-site destruction or use a reverse distributor (typically cleaner path
for researchers).

## 4. Procedure — on-site destruction

1. **Pre-destruction inventory** — weigh/measure the substance to be
   destroyed. Record in the destruction log:
   - Substance name and DEA schedule
   - Lot number
   - Quantity to be destroyed (with units)
   - Reason for destruction
   - Reference to the substance-specific running balance entry

2. **DEA Form 41 preparation** — complete all applicable fields prior
   to destruction. Both the destroyer and witness must be DEA registrants
   OR designated personnel on the registration.

3. **Destruction** — perform the approved non-retrievable method.
   The witness must observe the full destruction process.

4. **Post-destruction reconciliation** — update the substance-specific
   running balance sheet (see `04_sop_inventory.md` §4.5) to reflect
   the destroyed quantity.

5. **Form retention** — retain the completed DEA Form 41 at the
   facility for a minimum of two (2) years per 21 CFR 1304.04.

6. **DEA submission** — submit a copy of the completed DEA Form 41 to
   the DEA Diversion field office having jurisdiction within the
   timeframe specified on the form (typically immediately after
   destruction; verify current requirement with field office).

## 5. Procedure — reverse distributor

1. Identify a DEA-registered reverse distributor (list maintained at
   deadiversion.usdoj.gov)
2. Complete DEA Form 222 (for transfer of Schedule I/II) to document
   transfer to the reverse distributor
3. Package substance per reverse distributor's shipping requirements
4. Maintain shipping records and Form 222 copy on-site for 2 years
5. Upon receipt of destruction certificate from reverse distributor,
   file with the substance-specific records

## 6. Special circumstances

### 6.1 Theft or significant loss

If substance is unaccounted for and suspected stolen or lost, **do NOT
attempt destruction documentation** for the missing quantity. Instead,
immediately follow the Emergency Response SOP (`06_sop_emergency.md`)
and file DEA Form 106.

### 6.2 Expired substance retention

A substance may be kept beyond its manufacturer's expiration date solely
for destruction documentation purposes, provided it is stored in a
designated `quarantine` section of the Schedule I safe and labeled
`AWAITING DESTRUCTION — DO NOT USE`.
