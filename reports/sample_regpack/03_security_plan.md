## 1. Storage security

### Substance storage area

Dedicated 120 sq ft laboratory room with reinforced door (solid-core, deadbolt lock with restricted key control). Schedule I substances will be stored in a GSA Class 5 security container affixed to the building structural wall. The container is located against an interior wall not shared with any public corridor. Motion detectors cover the storage area and perimeter door; central station alarm monitoring is in place 24/7 via a UL 2050 Protection Level II certified provider.


### Required elements per 21 CFR 1301.72(a)

Schedule I substances must be stored in a **safe or steel cabinet**
that meets the following specifications:

- [ ] Construction: 750-lb minimum weight **OR** permanently affixed to
      the building structure (21 CFR 1301.72(a)(1))
- [ ] Lock: combination or key lock, with limited key control
- [ ] Location: within a securely locked, substantially constructed
      cabinet (21 CFR 1301.72(a)(2))
- [ ] Alarm: connected to central monitoring station OR local annunciator
      (21 CFR 1301.72(a)(3) permits 'adequate alarm system')

## 2. Access control (21 CFR 1301.75)

### Key / combination control

- Only the Principal Investigator and designated Alternate Responsible
  Individual shall hold keys or combinations to the Schedule I storage area.
- Combinations shall be changed upon personnel change, suspected
  compromise, and at minimum annually.
- A written log of key/combination assignments shall be maintained and
  made available upon DEA inspection.

### Authorized personnel list

A current roster of personnel authorized to access Schedule I substances
shall be maintained at the facility. Each authorized individual will:

- Complete a background check per 21 CFR 1301.90 — 21 CFR 1301.93
- Sign a statement acknowledging controlled substance handling policies
- Receive documented training on this security plan, applicable SOPs,
  and DEA reporting requirements

[LEGAL REVIEW] Background check scope varies by institution; many DEA-
registered research facilities use state-level criminal history checks.
Counsel should confirm what meets the 'questions related to... employee
theft and misuse' standard in 21 CFR 1301.90.

## 3. Record-keeping (21 CFR 1304)

All records required by 21 CFR 1304 shall be maintained on-site for a
minimum of two (2) years and made available for DEA inspection:

- **Initial inventory** — taken upon registration issuance (21 CFR 1304.11)
- **Biennial inventory** — every two years thereafter on the same date
- **Receipt records** — invoice from source for each acquisition
- **Dispensing records** — each time substance is dispensed from storage
  (who, how much, for what purpose, witness if applicable)
- **Destruction records** — DEA Form 41 for any destroyed substances
- **Transfer records** — if substance moves between DEA-registered
  facilities under common ownership

See `04_sop_inventory.md` and `05_sop_destruction.md` for detailed
procedures.

## 4. Personnel screening (21 CFR 1301.90-1301.93)

Employees with access to controlled substances must:

- Complete the questionnaire required by 21 CFR 1301.90
- Acknowledge that conviction of a felony drug offense may bar employment
  in controlled substance handling roles
- Undergo a background check commensurate with the institution's policy
  and the DEA standard

Records of these screenings shall be maintained in a personnel file
separate from the Schedule I operational records.

## 5. Emergency response

Theft, loss, or breach procedures are detailed in `06_sop_emergency.md`.
Key points:

- **Significant loss or theft** must be reported to the DEA within
  one business day on DEA Form 106 (21 CFR 1301.74(c) for
  non-practitioners; 21 CFR 1301.76(b) for practitioners)
- Local law enforcement must be notified for any suspected theft
- The Responsible Individual must document and preserve evidence
  pending DEA investigation

## 6. Facility access

### During operating hours

- All personnel entering the security area shall be visible to an
  authorized individual or monitored by the alarm system
- Visitors to the facility shall not enter the Schedule I storage area
  without escort and documentation in the visitor log

### Outside operating hours

- The Schedule I storage area shall be locked, alarmed, and accessible
  only to the Responsible Individual and Alternate Responsible Individual

## 7. Annual review

This security plan shall be reviewed annually by the Responsible
Individual and revised as needed for:

- Changes in facility physical layout
- Changes in authorized personnel
- Changes in Schedule I substances handled
- Changes in applicable DEA regulations

The reviewed-and-approved date shall be documented on this cover sheet.
